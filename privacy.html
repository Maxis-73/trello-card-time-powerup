Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 8, 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how the Trello Card Time Tracker Power-Up ("we", "our", or "the Power-Up") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use this Power-Up within Trello boards.

By using this Power-Up, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1. Card and List Data

The Power-Up collects and stores the following information locally within your Trello account:

  • Card IDs: To identify and track individual cards
  • List IDs and Names: To track which lists cards are moved to
  • Timestamps: Entry and exit dates when cards move between lists
  • List History: A chronological record of card movements between lists

2.2. Board Configuration

The Power-Up stores your preferences for which lists should be tracked:

  • List tracking states (enabled/disabled per list)
Important: All data collected by this Power-Up is stored locally within your Trello account using Trello's built-in storage API. We do not have access to your data, and it is not transmitted to any external servers.

3. How We Use Your Information

The information collected is used solely for the following purposes:

  • Calculating and displaying the time cards spend in each list
  • Showing the total time cards have been on the board
  • Maintaining a history of card movements between lists
  • Respecting your preferences for which lists should be tracked

4. Data Storage

4.1. Storage Location

All data is stored using Trello's Power-Up storage API, which means:

  • Data is stored within your Trello account
  • Data is associated with specific cards and boards
  • Data is subject to Trello's own privacy and security policies

4.2. Data Types

  • Card-level data: Stored as "shared" data, accessible to all Power-Ups on the card
  • Board-level data: Stored as "private" data, specific to this Power-Up

5. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not share, sell, or transmit your data to any third parties. All data remains within your Trello account and is only accessible through the Trello Power-Up API.

This Power-Up does not use any external analytics services, advertising networks, or third-party data processors.

6. Data Security

The security of your data is managed by Trello's infrastructure and security measures. This Power-Up:

  • Uses Trello's official Power-Up API for all data operations
  • Does not implement custom data transmission or storage mechanisms
  • Follows Trello's security best practices for Power-Ups

However, please note that no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Your Rights and Control

You have full control over the data collected by this Power-Up:

  • Disable tracking: You can disable tracking for specific lists through the Power-Up settings
  • Remove Power-Up: Removing the Power-Up from a board will stop data collection, but existing data may remain in Trello's storage
  • Data deletion: Data is stored per card and board. Deleting a card or board will remove associated data

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this Privacy Policy.

You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are posted on this page.

9. Compliance

This Power-Up is designed to comply with:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
  • Trello's Power-Up development guidelines

Since all data is stored locally within Trello and not transmitted externally, the Power-Up operates with minimal data processing and maximum user control.
